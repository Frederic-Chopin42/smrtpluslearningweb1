About SMRT+ Learning

Our approach in this hackathon is to assist people in finding an efficient method
that improves one’s experience with online learning while staying connected with others. 
On our website, we have a to-do list to help people organize, a posting section to connect 
people with posts and possibly news. We have a hotkeys section of our website that will 
help people quickly find ways to use their computers.


“A History Of SMRT+ From Nate Greco”

One day in a grade 5 Spanish classroom, a table group of four was formed. The members 
were: Brandon Phui, Isaac Wong, Ryln Mac, and last but not least the founder, 
Nate Greco. Nate and Brandon had completely no experience with Spanish except for 
knowing a few words. My friends helped me out as both Ryln and Isaac had taken Spanish 
before. The next year came along and new members wished to join. Their names were 
Mohommad Abdullah and Nathan He. Then, disaster struck. There were only five spots at 
the table group. Innovative members: Brandon Phui and Ryln Mac came up with a system 
based on grades to let members into the group. Nathan and Abdullah were then given an 
incentive to raise their Spanish grades to enter SMRT. That year, COVID-19 was introduced. 
We were faced with another challenge to stop the COVID-19 pandemic. As COVID-19 was raging 
in Alberta, we transitioned to online learning. The experience in online learning was 
described as horrible by many students. It was very disengaging for many students. 
Students struggled to learn and focus online. As SMRT entered the 7th grade, 
COVID-19 was widespread. They used sanitizer and masks to allow them to go to school 
and protect those around them. SMRT became more of an organization as it began to create 
alliances in competitive games in Physical Education and created friend groups. Once again,
we had to transition to online learning. This time, however, the online learning experience
was more structured, but students still found it difficult to connect with other peers and 
stay focused in class. SMRT had to expand and adapt now because members Shotaro Richardson
and Piaoyi Wu wished to join the group, so once again, innovative member Brandon Phui came
up with a new name, SMRT+. SMRT had previously stood for Spanish Management Regulations
Team but now has been extended further than Spanish class with SMRT+. SMRT was used only
in Spanish class whereas SMRT+ is inclusive, and not only just used in Spanish class. Over 
two years, SMRT has developed significantly and is still growing today.
